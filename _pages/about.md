      </p>

    <div class="section-heading">
      <p>Research Interests</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section-body">
      <p>
        My current work revolves around three threads: clinically grounded image understanding, robust and interpretable modeling, and practical evaluation for deployment.
        I am especially drawn to problems where models need to reason under uncertainty, distribution shift, or weak supervision.
      </p>
      <ul class="editorial-list">
        <li>Medical image analysis for diagnosis, prognosis, and decision support</li>
        <li>Robust computer vision under domain shift and limited annotation</li>
        <li>Explainable, trustworthy, and clinically meaningful AI systems</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="home-section">
    <div class="section-heading">
      <p>Current Direction</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section-body">
      <p>
        At the moment, I am interested in designing data-efficient learning strategies for medical imaging pipelines and in understanding when explanation methods genuinely help clinicians.
        The underlying question is less about adding another benchmark point and more about making vision models reliable enough to matter in practice.
      </p>
